• Packrats
    1. Dusky-footed rats (a.k.a. packrats) build homes out of sticks and mud.
    2. Homes are domes with a 4-5 foot diameter.
    3. Collect shiny objects and bring them back to their dens. These objects are called souvenirs.
    4. Only females live in dens
  • Western Fence Lizard
    1. Diurnal Reptiles
    2. Commonly seen sunning on paths, rocks, and fence posts, and other high places
    3. Protect themselves with their fast reflexes, including biting and defecating on their attacker
    4. Can change skin color from light grey to jet black. This is more commonly use for thermoregulation
    5. Like other reptiles, they hibernate in winter
  • African Clawed Frog
    1. Female frogs are larger than males
    2. Body is smooth and covered in mucus to protect thier sensitive skin
    3. Color of the body can be green, grey, brown or albino with dark blotches that provide camouflage against predators
    4. Has ability to change its color to adapt to environment
    5. Males do not have vocal cords. They make a clicking sound as their mating call
  • Barn Owl
    1. Nocturnal bird of prey
    2. White heart shaped face
    3. Females are larger than males
    4. Barn Owl's flight is silent
    5. They nest in hollow trees and females lay 2-11 eggs
    6. Fully grown barn owls are the size of a small cat and weigh only a pound
  • Wild Turkey
    1. Male turkeys are called "toms" or “gobblers” and female turkeys are called "hens"
    2. A turkey’s diet consists of seeds, nuts, fruit, insects, and small lizards
    3. Turkeys have a wingspan of up to six feet, which makes them the largest bird in their natural habitat
    4. Turkey calls are loud. On a clear, quiet day, a gobble made by a male turkey can be heard from a mile away
    5. Turkeys have no external ear structures, but they have small holes in their head located behind their eyes where sound can enter. Turkeys can pinpoint sounds up to a mile away
    6. Newly hatched turkeys leave the nest within 12 to 24 hours in order to feed. Mothers will feed the hatchlings for a short period before they must fend for themselves
  • Crab Spider
    1. Crab spider can reach 0.16 to 0.3 inches in length
    2. Females are larger than males
    3. Females are usually white, pale green or yellow colored and have two pairs of red dots on the abdomen
    4. Males are darker colored and have greenish white abdomen covered with brown stripes
    5. Crab spider is able to change the color of the body to blend with the colors of its environment
    6. Crab spider has wide, flattened body and 4 pairs of legs
    7. First pair of legs is longer and better developed compared to other legs
    8. Crab spiders have 8 eyes and ability to see in all directions
    9. Males have more slender body and longer legs
    10. The name "crab spider" refers to crab-like shape of the body and ability of these animals to walk sideways and backwards, just like crabs
    11. Crab spider is diurnal animal
    12. Crab spider is an ambush predator that silently waits in the flowers until the prey appears
    13. Main predators of crab spiders are wasps, ants, large spiders, lizards, birds and shrews
    14. Crab spider can survive one or more years in the wild, depending on the species and type of habitat
