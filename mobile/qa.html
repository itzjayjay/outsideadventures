• Q: What is the name of the rats that build houses?
  • A: Dusky-footed Woodrat (a.k.a. packrat)

  • Q: How big are their homes?
  • A: 4-5 foot diameter dome.

  • Q: What behavioral differences are there compared to other rats?
  • A: Packrats tend to collect "souvenirs" and bring them to their huts. They also live in colonies.

  • Q: What other extraordinary animals are there at Mission Trails?
  • A: All the wildlife here is extraordinary.

  • Q: What steps should a visitor take when spotting an animal in distress?
  • A: Call the visitor center or find a park ranger.

  • Q: How do the park rangers tend to injured animals?
  • A: They treat any wounds and take them to Project Wildlife.

  • Q: How often does a park visitor inform you of an injured animal?
  • A: We rarely get any calls about injured animals

  • Q: After a wildfire, what actions do the park rangers take to secure the surviving wildlife?
  • A: They treat any wounds and take them to Project Wildlife.
